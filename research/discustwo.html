Accessibility

For my demo app, I think I could change my HTML and make it more accessible by weeding out all the different classes and condensing things into one large section with a few divs/sections instead. Have the most important information at the top with the less important following in smaller font. I could also condense my different linked sections, perhaps put related subjects in one page instead of having a page for each.

Some common frustrations among screen readers are conflicts between the screen reader and some applications, confusion caused by page layout and poorly designed or unlabeled forms. Most of these frustrations can be fixed pretty simply by rebooting the users computer. Also, the web developer must be sure that they have all of the appropriate tags included in their mark up, and it is free of syntax errors. Page layout could be solved by limiting the amount of content trying to be displayed on the page, dividing the page accordingly with the most important information on the top and less relevant information following in a smaller font.
